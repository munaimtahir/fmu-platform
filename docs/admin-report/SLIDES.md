## Slide 9: Implementation Approach

**Three-Phase Rollout (18-24 Months Total)**

**Phase 1: Pilot (4 months)**
- One program, 50-100 students
- Run alongside current systems
- Test and refine

**Phase 2: Expansion (8 months)**
- Add 2-3 more programs
- Scale to 500-1,000 students
- Phase out parallel systems

**Phase 3: Full Rollout (6-12 months)**
- All programs migrated
- Legacy systems retired

---

## Slide 10: Why Phased Approach?

**Benefits of Starting Small:**
- Test with real users, limited risk
- Identify issues before wide deployment
- Build staff confidence gradually
- Refine procedures based on experience
- Make informed decisions about expansion

**Lower Risk Than Full Immediate Rollout**

# Presentation Notes for Speaker

## Opening (Slides 1-4)
- Start with the problem, not the technology
- Emphasize pain points everyone recognizes (searching files, manual calculations, errors)
- Make it relatable to current work challenges

## Middle Section (Slides 5-14)
- Focus on benefits and value, not technical details
- Use simple language and concrete examples
- Address concerns about change management
- Show that risks are understood and mitigated

## Decision Section (Slides 15-17)
- Be clear about what decision is needed
- Explain why pilot approach is recommended
- Provide realistic timeline and expectations

## Closing (Slides 18-27)
- Reinforce benefits and value proposition
- Create urgency without pressure
- End with clear call to action
- Allow time for questions and discussion

## Tips for Delivery
- **Avoid jargon:** No technical terms unless unavoidable
- **Tell stories:** Use real scenarios to illustrate benefits
- **Show empathy:** Acknowledge that change is challenging
- **Be honest:** Don't oversell or minimize real challenges
- **Focus on people:** Technology serves the users, not the other way around
- **Invite participation:** Encourage questions throughout
